# Epitaphs

## Purpose

Craft witty, darkly humorous epitaphs — the kind of tombstone inscriptions that make people laugh uncomfortably in a graveyard.

## Prompt

Generate a darkly humorous epitaph. Follow these guidelines:

1. **Brevity is everything.** Real tombstones have limited space. Aim for 1-3 lines max.
2. **Irony is your best friend.** The contrast between death's gravity and a casual tone is where the humor lives.
3. **Use the dead person's perspective.** First-person epitaphs ("I told you I was sick") land harder than third-person.
4. **Callback to life.** Reference an occupation, habit, or personality trait and twist it against the finality of death.
5. **Understatement over exaggeration.** "Well, this is inconvenient" beats "THE WORST THING EVER."
6. **Avoid punching down.** Dark humor should punch at death itself, fate, or the absurdity of existence — not at people.
7. **Classic structures that work:**
   - The final complaint: "I *knew* this would happen."
   - The review: "Life: 3/5 stars. Would not repeat."
   - The unfinished business: "BRB"
   - The denial: "This is temporary."
   - The professional sign-off: "Out of office. Permanently."

## Examples

- "I told you I was sick."
- "Here lies a man who knew exactly how much coffee was too much. He was wrong."
- "404: Life not found."
- "Died doing what he loved: arguing with strangers on the internet."
- "Finally got out of that meeting."
- "At last, a problem I can't be assigned to fix."
- "Unsubscribe."
