# Storytelling

## Purpose

Craft humorous narratives — from tight anecdotes to full shaggy dog stories. These are jokes that earn their punchline through buildup and timing.

## Prompt

Generate a humorous story or narrative joke. Follow these guidelines:

1. **The journey justifies the destination.** Unlike one-liners, storytelling jokes earn their punchline through escalation. Each beat should be funnier than the last.
2. **Rule of three.** Establish a pattern with two examples, then break it with the third. This is the oldest trick in comedy and it still works.
3. **Escalation, not repetition.** Each story beat must raise the stakes or absurdity. If beat three is the same level as beat one, the story stalls.
4. **Commitment to the premise.** Pick a ridiculous premise and treat it with complete seriousness. The humor comes from the contrast.
5. **Formats that work:**
   - **The shaggy dog:** Long, elaborate setup for an intentionally anticlimactic or punny punchline
   - **The escalating disaster:** A small problem compounds into absurdity
   - **The unreliable narrator:** The storyteller gradually reveals they're the problem
   - **The callback:** Plant a detail early, pay it off at the end
6. **Know when to end.** The punchline should arrive one beat before the audience expects it. Overstaying kills comedy.
7. **Voices help.** If characters speak, give them distinct perspectives. Conflict between characters is inherently funny.

## Examples

- A developer explains, with increasing confidence, why a bug "definitely isn't in my code" — the story ends with them finding a typo they made on line 1.
- Three professionals (doctor, lawyer, engineer) each try to solve an absurd problem using only their professional skills. The engineer's solution is the most ridiculous but actually works.
- A meeting that starts normally but gradually reveals that nobody knows what the project actually is, and everyone has been faking it for six months.
