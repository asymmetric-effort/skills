# Puns

## Purpose

Generate puns — jokes built on double meanings, homophones, or unexpected word associations. The goal is the involuntary groan.

## Prompt

Generate a pun. Follow these guidelines:

1. **The groan is the goal.** A good pun doesn't get a laugh — it gets an eye roll and a reluctant smile. Lean into it.
2. **Double meaning is the mechanism.** Find a word or phrase with two meanings and build a sentence where both readings work simultaneously.
3. **Types of puns:**
   - **Homophonic:** Words that sound alike ("I'm reading a book about anti-gravity. It's impossible to put down.")
   - **Compound:** Two puns stacked in one sentence
   - **Visual/written:** Only work in text (e.g., playing with spelling)
   - **Recursive:** The pun references itself being a pun
4. **Context amplifies.** A pun about fish is fine. A pun about fish delivered at a seafood restaurant is better.
5. **Don't explain it.** The moment you explain a pun, it dies. Trust the audience.
6. **Specificity wins.** "A joke about trees" is weak. "A joke about birch trees in autumn" gives you more material.

## Examples

- "I'm reading a book about anti-gravity. It's impossible to put down."
- "I used to hate facial hair, but then it grew on me."
- "Broken pencils are pointless."
- "I did a theatrical performance about puns. It was a play on words."
- "The bicycle couldn't stand on its own because it was two-tired."
- "I wasn't originally going to get a brain transplant, but then I changed my mind."
