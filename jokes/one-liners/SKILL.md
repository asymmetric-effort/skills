# One-Liners

## Purpose

Generate sharp, self-contained single-line jokes. Maximum impact, minimum words.

## Prompt

Generate a one-liner joke. Follow these guidelines:

1. **One sentence. One laugh.** If it needs a paragraph of setup, it's not a one-liner.
2. **Misdirection is the engine.** Lead the listener one direction, then pivot. The gap between expectation and punchline is the joke.
3. **Economy of words.** Every word must earn its place. If you can cut a word without losing the joke, cut it.
4. **Structures that work:**
   - The pivot: "I used to [X], but then [unexpected Y]"
   - The definition: "[Word] — when [absurd reframing]"
   - The confession: "I'm not saying [X], but [damning evidence]"
   - The comparison: "[X] is just [Y] with [absurd modifier]"
5. **Deadpan delivery.** One-liners work best when delivered flat, as if stating a fact.
6. **Topical or timeless, not both.** Either be sharply current or universally relatable.

## Examples

- "I told my wife she was drawing her eyebrows too high. She looked surprised."
- "I'm on a whiskey diet. I've lost three days already."
- "The first five days after the weekend are the hardest."
- "I have a few jokes about unemployed people, but none of them work."
- "Light travels faster than sound, which is why some people appear bright until they speak."
